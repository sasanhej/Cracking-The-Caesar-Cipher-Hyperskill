Description

Your friend is tired of the alphabet song. He went to a library, borrowed a book on ciphers, and found a much easier approach. Using two strips of paper, each one containing the alphabet a-z, he can produce the Caesar cipher with ease.

He can simply replace each top letter with the letter below by stacking the two pieces of paper and shifting one to the left or right before encoding.

A Caesar cipher with a left shift of 3 (source)

Objectives

In this stage, your program should:

  1. Take an input: A string of characters separated by spaces ' '
  2. Decipher the input. It has a left shift of 3
  3. Print the deciphered message
Examples

The greater-than symbol followed by a space (> ) represents the user input. Note that it's not part of the input.

Example 1:

> e b i i l
hello

Example 2:

> c o f b k a
friend

Example 3:

> h b v t l o a
keyword

Example 4:

> y r q q b o p z l q z e
butterscotch
